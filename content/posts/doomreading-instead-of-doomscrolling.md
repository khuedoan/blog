# Doomreading instead of doomscrolling

Bought this little guy, and it has been a game changer for me.

<!-- TODO image -->

Like many others, I have a tendency to pull out my phone at whatever moment
feels a little bit boring, trying to maximize productivity by checking email,
chat, news, or bug reports. It's kinda like a vicious cycle of dopamine hits:
each next one requires more dopamine than the last. I suddenly realized that I
can't even wait for my wife to quickly grab an item from a convenience store
without pulling out my phone anymore.

At the end of each "doomscrolling" session (which can span from 5 minutes to
even an hour), it feels empty. I can't really recall what I've read or watched.
I've come to the conclusion that trying to maximize productivity has
diminishing returns and is basically useless. However, it's extremely hard to
get out of the dopamine addiction. I've tried blocking apps and websites with
the OS's built-in features, but that seems to be ineffective because bypassing
them is nothing more than a minor inconvenience.

When I got the Xteink X4 (after much consideration about whether it's too
small), something clicked. I moved my phone to my right pocket, and put the X4
in my left pocket where my phone used to be. Now every time I have the muscle
memory trigger to pull out my phone, I pull out the X4 instead. It's so
frictionless to continue reading my books compared to my bigger ereaders (which
I'm still using). The small reading sessions of a few pages at a time compound
over the span of a few weeks, and I have finished another book today. While I
still haven't solved the tendency to try to do _something_ while I'm bored,
it's still a win because that time could have been wasted doomscrolling useless
content on Twitter or Reddit (even though they might still be technical -
they're still pretty shallow compared to the knowledge gained by book authors
after years of sweat and tears), and now it's used to read books instead. In my
childhood I've read basically 1 book a week (~52/year), but in the past 10
years it has dropped down close to 1-2/year. With the new pace unlocked by the
X4, I'm on a pretty good track to bump that up to ~20 books per year. And I
feel great every time I pull it out, instead of the guilt of wasting time by
pulling out my phone.

Besides the X4, there are some other alternatives from Boox, Bigme, or Viwoods,
but this one suits me better because I prefer a smaller device, and the limited
firmware is actually an upside, since I can't even do anything besides reading,
unlike other Android-based devices.

Ditch doomscrolling, doomreading instead!
